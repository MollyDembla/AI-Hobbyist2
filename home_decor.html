Patchwork Pillow

Material Needed:

  • Fabric scraps from old clothes
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread
  • Pillow insert or stuffing
  • Rotary cutter or scissors
  • Ruler
  • Iron

Instructions:

1. Select and Prepare Fabric:

  • Choose fabric pieces from old clothes with complementary colors and patterns.
  • Wash and iron the fabric to ensure it's clean and wrinkle-free.

2. Cut Fabric Squares:

  • Decide on the size of your pillow. For example, for a 16-inch pillow, you might use 4-inch squares.
  • Use scissors or a rotary cutter and ruler to cut equal-sized squares from your fabric.

3. Arrange the Squares:

  • Lay out the squares in a grid pattern, arranging them in a way that balances colors and patterns.

4. Sew the Squares Together:

  • Sew squares together row by row, placing them right sides together and stitching along the edges with a ¼-inch seam allowance.
  • Press the seams open or to one side with an iron.

5. Join the Rows:

  • Sew the rows together, matching seams carefully to ensure alignment.
  • Press the seams as before.

6. Add Backing Fabric:

  • Cut a piece of fabric for the back of the pillow, the same size as the patchwork front.
  • Place the front and back pieces right sides together and sew around the edges, leaving an opening to insert the pillow form.

7. Insert Pillow Form and Finish:

  • Turn the pillow cover right side out.
  • Insert the pillow form or stuffing.
  • Hand-sew the opening closed.

Door Mat

Material Needed:

  • Old t-shirts, sheets, or fabric scraps
  • Scissors
  • Needle and strong thread
  • Safety pins
  • Large safety pin or bodkin (for threading)

Instructions:

1. Prepare Fabric Strips:

  • Cut fabric into long strips, approximately 2 inches wide.
  • Join strips end-to-end by cutting small slits at the ends, threading one strip through the slit of another, and pulling tight to create a continuous length.

2. Start Braiding:

  • Take three strips, secure the top ends together with a safety pin, and attach it to a stable surface or anchor it under a heavy object.
  • Braid the strips together, keeping the tension even but not too tight.

3. Continue Braiding and Adding Strips:

  • As you near the end of a strip, add a new strip using the slit method described above.
  • Continue braiding until you have a substantial length; the longer the braid, the larger the rug.

4. Coil the Braid into a Rug Shape:

  • Start at one end of the braid and begin coiling it into a spiral or oval shape on a flat surface.
  • As you coil, sew the edges of the braid together using a needle and strong thread to secure the shape.

5. Finish the Rug:

  • Once all the braid is coiled and sewn into place, tuck the end of the braid under the rug and stitch it securely.
  • Trim any loose threads and ensure all parts are securely stitched.

Hanging Frame

Material Needed:

  • Wooden frame or embroidery hoop
  • Fabric with interesting patterns or textures
  • Scissors
  • Glue or staple gun

Instructions:

1. Select Fabric and Frame:

  • Choose a piece of fabric with a pattern or texture you’d like to display.
  • Select a frame or embroidery hoop that complements the fabric size.

2. Prepare the Fabric:

  • Iron the fabric to remove any wrinkles.
  • Cut the fabric to a size slightly larger than the frame or hoop.

3. Mount the Fabric:

  • For a frame: Lay the fabric over the frame backing, ensuring the design is centered.
  • Pull the fabric taut and secure it to the backing using glue or a staple gun.
  • Place the backing into the frame.
  • For an embroidery hoop: Loosen the outer ring and place the fabric over the inner ring.
  • Position the fabric as desired and tighten the outer ring over it, pulling the fabric taut as you go.

4. Trim Excess Fabric:

  • After securing, trim any excess fabric from the edges for a clean finish.

Curtains

Material Needed:

  • Assorted fabric scraps or old textiles
  • Measuring tape
  • Fabric scissors
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Curtain rod and hanging hardware

Instructions:

1. Measure Your Window:

  • Determine the desired length and width of your curtains.
  • Add extra inches to account for hems and seam allowances.

2. Select and Prepare Fabric:

  • Choose fabric pieces that complement each other in color and texture.
  • Wash and iron the fabrics to prevent shrinkage and ensure smooth sewing.

3. Design the Layout:

  • Arrange the fabric pieces in a pattern or randomly for a patchwork effect.
  • Ensure the combined width matches your window measurement plus allowances.

4. Sew the Fabric Pieces Together:

  • Place two fabric pieces with right sides facing each other.
  • Sew along the edge with a ½-inch seam allowance.
  • Continue adding pieces until you reach the desired width.
  • Press the seams open with an iron for a neat finish.

5. Hem the Edges:

  • Fold the side edges over ½ inch, then another ½ inch, and press.
  • Sew along the inner fold to create a clean hem.
  • Repeat for the bottom edge, folding over ½ inch twice, pressing, and sewing.

6. Create the Rod Pocket:

  • At the top edge, fold over ½ inch and press.
  • Then fold over again by 2-3 inches (depending on your curtain rod's size) and press.
  • Sew along the inner fold to form the rod pocket.
