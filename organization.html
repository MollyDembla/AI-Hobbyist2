Fabric Baskets

Material Needed:

  • Sturdy fabric (old jeans, canvas, etc.)
  • Thread
  • Scissors
  • Sewing machine or hand-sewing kit
  • Interfacing (optional for added structure)

Instructions:

1. Cut the Fabric:

  • Measure and cut two pieces of fabric for the basket’s sides (e.g., 10" x 10" for square baskets or a longer strip for a round one).

2. Cut Bottom Piece:

  • Cut a matching piece for the bottom of your basket.

3. Sew the Sides:

  • With the right sides of the fabric facing each other, sew the sides together to form a tube.

4. Attach the Bottom:

  • Pin the bottom piece to the tube, making sure to align the edges, and sew it into place.

5. Reinforce the Sides:

  • If you want more stability, add interfacing between the fabric layers.

6. Finish the Edges:

  • Fold over the top edge and sew it down for a clean finish.

7. Add Handles (Optional):

  • Cut strips of fabric for handles, fold, sew them into loops, and attach to the basket.

Hanging Organizers

Material Needed:

  • Fabric (old shirts, denim, or scraps)
  • Scissors
  • Sewing machine or hand-sewing kit
  • Measuring tape
  • Ribbon or dowel for hanging

Instructions:

1. Cut the Fabric:

  • Decide on the size of your organizer and cut one large piece for the backing (e.g., 12" x 24").

2. Make Pockets:

  • Cut smaller pieces for the pockets. Sew the edges of each pocket piece to prevent fraying, then attach them to the backing piece in a row.

3. Sew the Sides:

  • Fold the top edge of the backing fabric down and sew it to create a pocket for the dowel or ribbon.

4. Attach the Hanging Method:

  • Thread a dowel or sew on a ribbon to the top of the organizer for easy hanging.

5. Add Decorative Details:

  • Optionally, you can add buttons, ribbons, or other embellishments to make it more personal.

Drawer Dividers

Material Needed:

  • Fabric scraps or old clothes
  • Scissors
  • Cardboard or foam board
  • Glue or fabric tape
  • Ruler

Instructions:

1. Measure the Drawer:

  • Measure the dimensions of your drawer to determine the size of the dividers.

2. Cut the Fabric:

  • Cut your fabric into strips that are wide enough to cover the cardboard or foam board pieces.

3. Prepare the Dividers:

  • Cut the cardboard or foam board to the size of your drawer compartments.

4. Cover with Fabric:

  • Glue or tape the fabric onto the cardboard or foam board. Ensure the fabric covers all edges.

5. Assemble the Dividers:

  • Arrange the fabric-covered dividers inside the drawer to create compartments. Use glue or fabric tape to secure them.

Fabric-Covered Storage Boxes

Material Needed:

  • Cardboard boxes
  • Fabric (old t-shirts, curtains, etc.)
  • Scissors
  • Hot glue gun or fabric glue
  • Ribbon, buttons, or embellishments (optional)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Box:

  • Take an old cardboard box and ensure it’s clean and in good shape.

2. Cut the Fabric:

  • Cut fabric to fit the sides and bottom of the box, leaving an extra inch for folding over the edges.

3. Apply the Fabric:

  • Use fabric glue or a hot glue gun to attach the fabric to the cardboard, smoothing out wrinkles as you go.

4. Finish the Edges:

  • Fold over the fabric edges and glue them down to create a clean finish.

5. Add Decorative Details:

  • Optionally, add ribbon, buttons, or other embellishments to personalize the box.
